# rosette — references

Third piece in the 2swap basin/fractal series. Research lives in:

- `brainstorming/inspirations/2swap-refs.md` — the artist study.
- `brainstorming/techniques/basins-of-attraction.md` — Recipe 2
  (Newton). rosette is the **moving-roots** variant: roots stay on
  the unit circle but their *count* (n) changes with audio.
- `brainstorming/critiques/watershed-v1.md` — sibling Newton piece
  (moving cursor/keyboard roots). Chef-doeuvre.
- `brainstorming/critiques/shoal-v2.md` — sibling chaos piece. The
  "animate the landscape" lesson from shoal applies directly: with
  audio-driven n, the polynomial itself is the animated landscape.

Direct anchor: 2swap, *Mystery of the Quintic* and the
[Littlewood Polynomial Fractal Explorer](https://2swap.github.io/LittlewoodFractal/)
— both feature the n-th-roots-of-unity / polynomial root visual.

## The music

**Jon Hopkins — "Open Eye Signal"** (*Immunity*, 2013). 8 minutes,
~125 BPM, 4/4. The arc: a single quiet pulse, then layers stack
gradually for ~3 minutes; a glitchy break; the drop at ~4:00
unleashes the full pattern; a second build; a long, drifting outro.
The build IS the song. Maps directly to n growing through the build
and mass-snapping at the drop. Percussive enough for clean
downbeat-anchored geometric snaps.

Picked over Nils Frahm "Says" (also a beautiful build, but more
gradual and less percussive — would underplay the downbeat snaps).
