## Zach Lieberman — "Ink watercolor simulation again"
- <https://x.com/zachlieberman/status/2064533010838438310>
- Already in `generative-artists.md` + `vj-artists.md` as a practice
  (daily sketches, oF). The fresh, specific move is the *watercolor look*.
- **The surprise from research:** his fluid look is **not** a physical
  watercolor solver. From his own 2025 retrospective: *"images that feel
  like water simulation, like milk in coffee... created just using
  displacement after displacement in shader code."* Iterated domain
  warping / ping-pong feedback, then the paint cues added on top.
- **Steal:** displacement-feedback for the bleed + a cheap edge-darkening
  post (`pow(color, 1 + max3(color - blur(color))·k)`) to flip it from
  "dye/smoke" to "watercolour". → `techniques/watercolor-ink.md`,
  `pieces/ink-bloom.md`.

## Strano (@5tr4n0) — "Kinetic energy" (shared by @Inspector_9)
- <https://x.com/Inspector_9/status/2064413823738486917>
- Marcel Giannoccaro, Montréal. "Sculpting light, breaking pixels,
  plotting lines — mostly gif art." Chiaroscuro, cream-on-near-black,
  looping GIFs, restrained palette. Energy reads through **luminance +

- **Steal:** velocity-as-light. Map `speed²` (literal kinetic energy) to

  contrast rule. → `pieces/kinetic-energy.md`,
  `techniques/polyrhythmic-motion.md`.

## Kat / the Poet Engineer (@poetengineer__) — "luminous"
- <https://x.com/poetengineer__/status/2064370218470699292>
- Kat Zhang, Brooklyn. Live A/V; "abstracted data signals + poetic
  figuration." TouchDesigner + GLSL + MediaPipe hand-tracking. Treats
  **text as a manipulable spatial material** ("shaping a poem with a
  gesture"), glowing on near-black.
- **Steal two things:** (1) light-as-subject — emission written straight
  to the buffer, brightness *is* the form, HDR→bloom→tonemap so cores
  bleed; (2) typography as a *mask/emission field* (MSDF distance drives
  a generative interior + edge-glow), threshold coupled to audio/cursor
  for phase-lock. → `techniques/luminous-bloom.md`,
  `pieces/luminous-verse.md`.
