# Shadertoy & procedural references

Seeded from memory while WebSearch was unavailable. URLs to verify in a
future research pass. Creator handles are as I remember them.

## Domain warping & fbm mastery

- **Iñigo Quílez (iq)** — the canonical source for domain warping,
  distance estimators, palette cosine, signed distance functions. Any of
  his Shadertoy pieces is worth reading as a lesson. His blog posts
  (iquilezles.org/articles) are the reference material I return to most.
- **BigWings / Martijn Steinrucken** — long-form tutorials on Shadertoy
  that double as beautiful pieces. Good for seeing how production
  shader craft is organised. YouTube channel "The Art of Code".

## Hyperbolic / {p,q} tilings

- **Reinder Nijhoff** — has posted both Escher-style hyperbolic tilings
  and Apollonian packings on Shadertoy. Worth studying for how the
  tile-index lookup + iteration cap are structured.
- Search Shadertoy for `hyperbolic` — several creators have tackled
  {7,3} and {8,3} with varying success.

## Glitch & digital corruption

- **dila** — posted several analog-TV / VHS style distortion shaders.
  Block displacement, scanline warping, chroma bleed. Good reference
  for how to make glitch feel analog-warm rather than clinical-digital.
- **Flockaroo** — a lot of feedback-based, post-processy shaders that
  shade toward glitch without being glitchy per se.

## Kaleidoscopic motion

- **XorDev** — consistently posts short, beautiful pieces with unusual
  geometric moves. Worth a general browse for kaleidoscopic variation.
- Search `kaleido` on Shadertoy — surprising range.

## Space-filling curves

- **lsdlive** / **Kali** — both have posted Hilbert-distance-based
  pieces. Kali especially for the "path as primary structure" idea.

## Long-form variation / live visuals

- **Cyberspaceship** (?) — I recall pieces that build structure over
  minutes rather than seconds. Worth checking for how to compose at a
  long time-scale.
