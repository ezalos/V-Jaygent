# fiebre-de-amor — inspirations & references

Track: Havana D'Primera — "Fiebre de Amor", album *Será Que Se Acabó*
(2021). 16-piece, brass-forward timba orchestra led by Cienfuegos
trumpeter/singer **Alexander Abreu**. 4:51, C major, ~92 BPM.

## The single move to lift, per reference

- **Harmonograph (pendulum-drawn coprime-sinusoid curves, 1840s–1890s)** —
  the canonical device. Two/three damped pendulums at coprime frequency
  ratios trace a quasi-periodic rose. The move: *let the beat-frequencies
  BE the polyrhythm*; drift the ratios so the curve never retraces.
  <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harmonograph>
- **Godfried Toussaint, *The Geometry of Musical Rhythm*** — the clave as a
  necklace of pulses on a circle; the son/rumba clave are famous Euclidean
  rhythms. The move: the conductor clock is honest rhythm-geometry, not a
  spectrum ring. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euclidean_rhythm>
- **Timba structural devices** (timba.com encyclopedia) — the move set the
  section machine steals:
  - **Gears / "arriba"** — discrete switchable rhythm states the band snaps
    between. → switchable visual *vocabulary* per section (not re-shaded).
    <https://www.salsaforums.com/threads/timba-gears-and-how-to-dance-to-each-of-them-differently.39264/>
  - **Bloque** — whole band stops, then re-enters in unison syncopation.
    Suspense → exhilaration. → freeze the figure in the breakdown, slam on
    bass re-entry. The can't-miss screen event = genuine unpredictability.
    <https://www.timba.com/encyclopedia_pages/the-art-of-the-bloque>
  - **Despelote** — extreme quiet↔loud dynamic-range swings. → drain the
    frame then over-saturate. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timba>
  - **Clave 2-3 rumba** (edgier than salsa's son clave) + full trap kick on
    top of congas/timbales/bongó = heavier drive. Bass *tumbao* interlocks
    with piano *montuno* = the accelerating lattice the pens trace.
- **Song shape** — cuerpo (quiet sung verse, "marcha abajo") → montuno
  (long intense back half: coros, mambos, gears, "marcha arriba").
  <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salsa_(musical_structure)>

## Palette — "fiebre" as a warm-arc hue climb

Emotional temperature: HOT — feverish, molten, glowing, brass-bright,
sweaty. Taken as a **warm-arc drift** (V-Jaygent warm rule; cyan/mint
REJECTED):

- golden-hour Havana patina: cream / amber-gold `#FFE060` / mustard /
  terracotta-copper `#572900` (intro)
- fever-heat: tangerine `#F78E1E`, ember-crimson `#FF3E42`, body-red (climb)
- sweat-and-brass glint: bastard-amber stage wash, polished-gold speculars
  for the horn stabs (throughout)
- peak: hot-pink / magenta-rose `#FE6DC6` (in-arc ~320°) on near-black
- cierre: cool the fever toward deep wine / violet (still warm arc)

REJECTED from the research (cool intrusions, would fail warm_arc + VISION):
mint/turquoise/aqua Havana patina, Miami-Vice neon-cyan. Took only the warm
half. <https://www.color-hex.com/color-palette/23047> ·
<https://colorstories.substack.com/p/the-soft-warm-glow-of-bastard-amber>

## Arc (one line)
smoulder (golden-hour) → ignite on the coro → climb in density+heat through
the montuno → flash hot-pink on the mambo stabs → freeze-and-slam on the
bloque → break the fever to deep wine at the cierre.
