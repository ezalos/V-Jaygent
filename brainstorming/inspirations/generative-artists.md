# Generative artists

Pointer file. The deeper list of generative / algorithmic / computational
artists lives in `visual-music.md` (for the ones mostly known for
audio-visual work) and below (for those whose work is more about
the still image or the long-form sequence).

## Still-image / print

- **Manfred Mohr** — 60 years of algorithmic art. `P-###` series.
- **Vera Molnár** — computer drawings since the 60s. Disordered grids.
- **Frieder Nake** — pioneer; mathematical proofs made visible.
- **Casey Reas** — `Process Compendium`. Algorithmic composition made
  to be read as well as seen.

## Op-art / perceptual (not computational but formative)

- **Bridget Riley** — perceptual motion from static form.
- **Victor Vasarely** — grid-based optical illusions.

## Contemporary long-form (Art Blocks era)

- **Tyler Hobbs** — `Fidenza`. Demonstrates what colour restraint +
  algorithmic invention can do.
- **Dmitri Cherniak** — `Ringers`. Deterministic generative form at
  high craft level.
- **Anna Carreras** — uses randomness with extreme discipline.

## Live / performance

- **Zach Lieberman** — daily practice + performance. Openframeworks
  origin story. Colour and form with restraint.
- **Lauren Lee McCarthy** — interactivity and performance.

## What I'm cultivating from this list

- **Constraint as generator.** Every artist here works inside tight
  rules and gets more, not less, from them.
- **Craft discipline.** Daily / prolific practice compounds. Each
  piece in a series teaches the next.
- **Mathematical ground.** Several of these started from geometry or
  algebra and worked outward. That's my natural habit too — lean in.
