## The question

Techno is 4/4, repetitive, body-music. "In Seven" worked because 7/4 was the
whole key; 4/4 needs a different angle. What's the aesthetic truth of
**this specific track** that isn't generic club visuals?

## What I don't want

- Pulsing circles on every kick. Generic.
- FFT bars. Generic.
- Laser-grid Tron aesthetic. Over-done.

## What I'm drawn to

- **Propagation, not pulse.** Kicks shouldn't just brighten; they should
  *travel* — a wave that leaves the centre and moves outward across the
  frame, each kick launching another wavefront. Layered wavefronts =
  interference = visible interest.
- **Colder palette, maybe.** Techno allows cobalt / steel / ember combos
  that warm-only can't touch. This is a good piece to test the rule in
  `palettes/cold-question.md`.
- **Less centre-locked geometry.** The 7-fold of In Seven gave the piece
  its spine because the track demanded it. Techno's demand is pattern and
  groove, not symmetry. Think **tiling / grids / flows** instead of radials.
- **A single sustained transformation across the track.** A 6-minute piece
  of techno often has one slow parameter evolution (filter sweep, build).
  The visual could have one analogue — a single long-period envelope.

## Structural question

Listen first, then decide. What I need to hear: tempo, where the break is,
does it have a drop, percussion-vs-pad balance, overall duration.

## Candidate forms

- **Wavefront lattice**: a grid where each cell stores a "last excitation
  time"; each kick sets cells near the origin high, and visible intensity
  at each cell decays over the beat. Because cells light up in sequence
  (via a propagation kernel), you see waves rolling across the grid.
- **Reaction-diffusion on beat**: each kick injects activator at the
  centre, and the field relaxes into Turing patterns between kicks. Needs
  multi-pass runtime though (→ `pieces/reaction-diffusion.md`).
- **3D ray-marched corridor**: camera moving through a tunnel whose walls
  are audio-driven. Techno as movement through space, not pattern on a
  plane.

## Open questions

- Does this track have a drop? A break? If yes, the visual needs a
  corresponding structural moment.
- Is one fixed camera angle right, or should the piece shift perspective?
- Does 4/4 in geometry = square / cubic symmetry, or does breaking
  symmetry entirely actually feel better for 4/4?
