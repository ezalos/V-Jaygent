2 · The decision process, in order

The order matters — each stage can short-circuit the rest:

A  CLAIM CHECK (binary gate, pre-scoring)
   restate meta.yaml's thesis → do the frames deliver it?
   FAIL ⇒ that IS the top fix, whatever the scores say. "Fix the lie first."

B  MESMERIZING PROBES (5× pass/fail/weak)
   eye-landing · prediction · squint · hue-drift · mystery
   prediction = HARD GATE: fail ⇒ verdict structural-rethink, full stop.
   ≤3/5 ⇒ structural-rethink (no dimension-polish can save it).

C  CONDITIONAL PROBE FAMILIES (only those meta.yaml makes applicable)
   interaction 7 · music 4 · song-level 6 · dual-input 7 · layered 8–11
   each probe: pass/fail/weak — or shader-pass/-fail/-unclear when only
   the GLSL can answer (critic must cite the deciding line).
   Family tallies route the top_fix (e.g. interaction ≤3/7 ⇒ the fix
   must address interaction).

D  DIMENSIONS (6× scored 1–5 — INDEPENDENT of probes)
   judged directly against written 1/3/5 anchors in taste.md §Dimensions;
   2 and 4 are "between"; ambiguity rounds DOWN.

E  VERDICT (one of five, bars in the glossary)
   reconciled against the critic's own data — calibration rules forbid
   rounding up ("if your notes say 'predictable', prediction is fail").

To your question "are the dimension numbers drawn from the other grades?" — no. Probes and dimensions are two independent axes over the same evidence. Probes are binary-ish questions ("can you predict the next 10 seconds?"); dimensions are quality scales ("how good is the motion, 1–5, against these anchors?"). They meet only at the verdict, which has bars on both (e.g. chef-d'oeuvre = 5/5 probes and all testable dims ≥ 4). The "avg dims 4.5/5" line in the studio is a display-only mean computed by studio/server.mjs compositeOf() — it exists in the UI, not in the rubric.

3 · Outputs — the contract

One markdown document, critiques/<slug>-vN.md, with required prose sections (claim · frame-by-frame table · per-family probe arguments · dimensions table · what's working · what's imperfect · verdict + justification) ending in a fenced YAML tail:

piece, iteration, verdict, claim_check
mesmerizing_passes + mesmerizing_probes{...}
<family>_passes + <family>_probes{...}     # omitted when n/a
scores{palette_cohesion … form_ending}
top_fix{dimension, what, why, caution}       # REQUIRED iff needs-tweak
evidence[ evidence/<slug>-vN/<frame>.png … ]

Three consumers parse this: the iterate loop (routes on verdict, applies top_fix.what, protects top_fix.caution), the studio grades view (chips, panels, tooltips, evidence strip), and the next critique (reads it to stay consistent). Change the schema → change all three.

5 · Check yourself

Q1. A piece scores 4/5 mesmerizing, but the failing probe is Prediction. What verdict must the critic return?

Prediction failing forces structural-rethink regardless of the other passes — unpredictability is a precondition, not a tradeoff dimension (taste.md §Decision).

Q2. What does shader-pass mean on a probe?

Some probes (reversibility, latency…) need interactions headless capture can't perform. The critic reads the code instead and must cite the deciding line. It counts as a pass in the N/M tallies. (vjay-iterate §~376)

Q3. Where do the 1–5 dimension scores come from?

Independent axis: each dimension has 1/3/5 anchor descriptions; ambiguity rounds down. The only computed number is the UI's "avg dims" mean (compositeOf() in server.mjs), which is display-only.

Q4. Claim check fails, but every dimension scores 4–5. What is the top fix?

"A failed claim check is the top fix, regardless of how the 1–5 scores come out. Fix the lie first. Polish second." (taste.md §claim check). Note option C is a real temptation — and it's the premise-wrong escape hatch, which only Louis can approve.

Primary source to read next: taste.md §"Mesmerizing — the first question" (the five probes, especially the two-timescale Prediction test) — it is the best-written part of the rubric and the part you'll touch first when improving the critic.
